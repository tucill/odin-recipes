Fried Chicken

Description

Fried chicken is a popular dish made by frying seasoned chicken pieces until golden brown.

The chicken has a cispy outer layer while remaining juicy and tender on the inside. With its savory flavor and satisfying crunch, fried chicken is a favorite for family meals and gathering.

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • All-purpose flour
  • Cornstarch
  • Garlic powder
  • Salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • Paprika
  • Eggs
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Clean the chicken pieces and pat them dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper, then let them marinate for about 15-30 minutes.
  2. Make the Coating: In a bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, paprika, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Prepare the Egg Wash: In another bowl, beat the eggs until well combined
  4. Coat the chicken: Dip each chicken piece into the egg wash, allowing excess to drip off, then dredge it in the flour mixture until fully coated. Press lightly to ensure the coating sticks.
  5. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet or deep fryer, heat enough cooking oil over medium heat until it reaches about 350 degrees Celsius
  6. Fry the Chicken: Carefully place the coated chicken pieces in the hot oil. Fry them in batches to avoid overcrowding. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Drain and Serve: Once cooked, remove the chicken and place it on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce or sides.
